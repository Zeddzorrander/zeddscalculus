\begin{exercise}

Suppose $\{a_n\}_{n=1}$ is a sequence defined by the formula:

\[a_n = 5-6n+n^2\]

Write down $a_1$, $a_2$, $a_3$, $a_4$, and $a_5$.

    \begin{align*}
      a_1 &= \answer[given]{0} & 
      a_2 &= \answer[given]{-3} & 
      a_3 &= \answer[given]{-4} & 
      a_4 &= \answer[given]{-3} & 
      a_5 &= \answer[given]{0} 
    \end{align*}

\begin{exercise}    
Thus, the rule above generates the ordered list:

\[
\answer{0} ,\answer{-3},\answer{-4},\answer{-3},\answer{0}, \dots
\]   

\end{exercise}
\end{exercise}
